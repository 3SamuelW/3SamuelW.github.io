01. About Me

I'm Houlin Wang, an undergraduate at Zhejiang University majoring in Biomedical Engineering (Instrumentation) with a minor in Computer Science. GPA 4.58/5.0, passionate about Brain-Computer Interfaces, medical image analysis, and multimodal AI.

I previously participated in the ZJU TruSense biosensor international competition team, responsible for circuit design and PCB layout. I also contributed to a deep learning-based corneal confocal microscopy diagnosis project, focusing on multimodal model training and data processing. I am currently involved in neural signal analysis research at Qiushi Academy for Advanced Studies, ZJU, exploring non-emotional memory encoding mechanisms between the hippocampus and amygdala.

Beyond research, I enjoy language learning (Chinese, English, French, Spanish), swimming, football, and open-source knowledge sharing.

02. Research Interests

My research spans the intersection of neural engineering, medical imaging, and artificial intelligence.

BCI & Neural Signal Analysis

Researching non-emotional memory encoding mechanisms between the hippocampus and amygdala, exploring neuronal firing patterns and cross-region coordination, with a focus on BCI applications in fundamental neuroscience and medical rehabilitation.

EEG/ECoG Hippocampus Memory Encoding Neural Decoding

Medical Image Analysis

Building deep learning models for ophthalmic image classification and segmentation, enabling high-throughput real-time diagnosis of corneal lesions.

Segmentation CLIP Confocal Microscopy

Multimodal AI

Fusing image and text representations to build cross-scenario intelligent interaction platforms, exploring vision-language models in medical applications.

Vision-Language PyTorch Multimodal Fusion

Biosensors & Instrumentation

Designing portable sensor systems for physiological parameter detection, combining circuit design and signal processing to address real-world healthcare challenges.
